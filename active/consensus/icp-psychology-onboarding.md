# Stochastic Multi-Agent Consensus Report &mdash; ICP Psychology + Onboarding Flow

**Problem**: Design the sales-to-onboarding flow based on the ICP&apos;s deepest perceived pain, fastest time-to-value, and natural value stacking
**Agents**: 10
**Date**: 2026-04-03

---

## The One-Paragraph Summary

Alberta renovation contractors feel missed calls most acutely (10/10 intensity) &mdash; every contractor has a specific memory of a $30K+ job lost because they were on a roof. This pain SELLS. But dead quote recovery RETAINS &mdash; the moment a $40K kitchen job they&apos;d written off texts back saying &quot;we&apos;re still interested,&quot; the contractor becomes a believer and a referrer. The optimal flow leads with missed calls (visceral demo), captures payment in the emotional peak, then fires outbound quote reactivation same-day so the contractor wakes up to recovered money. Each resolved pain reveals the next: missed calls handled &rarr; &quot;what about my dead quotes?&quot; &rarr; quotes recovering &rarr; &quot;what about new estimates going silent?&quot; &rarr; estimate follow-up running &rarr; &quot;I should have more reviews&quot; &rarr; review automation active. The referral story writes itself: &quot;I got a $40K kitchen back from a lead I&apos;d given up on. I didn&apos;t do anything.&quot;

## Part 1: The Pain Hierarchy

### Pain 1 &mdash; &quot;I miss calls and lose jobs&quot; (10/10 intensity, 8/10 agents rank it #1)

**How they feel it:** Visceral, daily, specific. They have a memory of THE call &mdash; the $45K basement that went to voicemail on a Saturday. They feel it in their chest.

**Language they use:**
- &quot;I lose jobs every week just because I can&apos;t pick up the phone&quot;
- &quot;By the time I call back, they&apos;re gone&quot;
- &quot;My competitor &mdash; I don&apos;t know how he answers every call&quot;
- &quot;I had a $45,000 basement call me on a Saturday, went to voicemail. Never heard from them again.&quot;

**Tried to solve it?** Yes. Wife answers sometimes. Part-time office person who doesn&apos;t work weekends. Calling back faster. None of it works consistently.

**Why it&apos;s #1 for SELLING:** It converts a real memory of lost money into felt urgency. They can name a specific job and dollar amount. The pain is not abstract.

---

### Pain 2 &mdash; &quot;My quotes go silent&quot; (8/10 intensity, chronic)

**How they feel it:** Demoralizing, slow burn. They spent 3 hours measuring, writing a quote, driving back, emailing a PDF. Then silence. They assume the homeowner went cheaper.

**Language they use:**
- &quot;I send quotes, never hear back&quot;
- &quot;They just ghost you&quot;
- &quot;I follow up once and then I feel like I&apos;m bugging them&quot;
- &quot;I spent 3 hours on that quote, never even got a response&quot;

**Why they don&apos;t follow up (Agent 4, contrarian insight):** It&apos;s not laziness &mdash; it&apos;s fear of rejection dressed up as pride. The estimate represents their judgment and worth. Chasing it makes them feel like a salesman, which feels beneath them as a craftsman.

**Why it&apos;s #1 for RETENTION:** When the system reactivates a dead quote and the homeowner says &quot;actually we&apos;re still interested&quot; &mdash; that&apos;s found money. The contractor didn&apos;t have to beg. The system did it for them. This is the moment that locks retention and triggers referrals.

---

### Pain 3 &mdash; &quot;I got burned before&quot; (9/10 intensity, but NOT sellable)

This is a TRUST WOUND, not a pain to solve. It&apos;s a filter every pitch must pass through.

**The specific pattern (Agent 4):**
1. Agency promises leads. Contractor gets leads. Leads are garbage.
2. Agency shows &quot;47 leads this month!&quot; Contractor closes zero.
3. Agency says &quot;it&apos;s a conversion problem&quot; &mdash; shifting blame.
4. Contractor feels stupid for not being able to close. Cancels.

**Language:** &quot;I tried that before, total waste of money.&quot; / &quot;These guys promise the world and deliver nothing.&quot; / &quot;My buddy did the same thing, got screwed too.&quot;

**How to neutralize (not sell against):**
- Month-to-month, cancel anytime (say this EARLY)
- Guarantee before price, every time
- Show results before asking for trust
- One person, not a company &mdash; &quot;I set it up, I manage it, text me directly&quot;

## Part 2: The Behavioral Psychology

### What the Contractor is REALLY Thinking (Agent 8)

**Five questions running through their head during every pitch:**
1. Is this person going to waste my time and money like the last agency?
2. Does this person actually understand what my business is?
3. Is this going to make me look more professional or less?
4. Is this going to require me to manage another thing?
5. Can I get out if it doesn&apos;t work?

**The contractor who buys is the one where you answered all five before they had to ask.**

### Cognitive Biases to Leverage

| Bias | How to Use It |
|------|--------------|
| **Loss aversion** | Frame as recovering what they already own, not buying something new. &quot;You&apos;ve got 20 leads that never booked. We recover those first.&quot; |
| **Availability heuristic** | Surface a specific memory. &quot;Think about the last call you missed. What was that worth?&quot; |
| **Social proof** | Lead with a named peer story. &quot;Mike in Red Deer &mdash; 4-man crew &mdash; recovered a $14K re-roof from a dead lead.&quot; |
| **Reciprocity** | Do something valuable before asking. Audit their lead list live on the call. |
| **Immediacy bias** | Pitch 72-hour results, not annual ROI. |

### Biases to Neutralize

| Bias | How to Neutralize |
|------|------------------|
| **Status quo** | &quot;This doesn&apos;t replace what you&apos;re doing. It runs underneath it, on the leads that fall through the cracks anyway.&quot; |
| **Agency anchoring** | &quot;That was pay-per-lead &mdash; buying strangers. This is re-activation &mdash; working leads that already know you.&quot; |
| **Competence threat** | &quot;The follow-up gap isn&apos;t a contractor problem &mdash; it&apos;s a capacity problem. You can&apos;t be texting leads from a roof.&quot; |
| **Identity threat** | Never say &quot;automated&quot; or &quot;AI.&quot; Say &quot;your leads get followed up on even when you&apos;re mid-job.&quot; |

### The Commitment Ladder (Agent 8)

| Rung | What You Ask | Cost to Them |
|:----:|-------------|:------------:|
| 1 | &quot;Fair to say there are leads that never booked but might have?&quot; | Zero |
| 2 | &quot;Can I show you something? 90 seconds.&quot; | 90 seconds |
| 3 | Free audit: &quot;You&apos;ve got ~X leads dormant. That&apos;s ~$Y sitting there.&quot; | Zero (you did the work) |
| 4 | &quot;Want to see what your customers would receive?&quot; | 5 minutes |
| 5 | Guarantee + price: &quot;If we don&apos;t recover $1,000 in 30 days, month 2 is free.&quot; | Decision |
| 6 | &quot;When works for a 30-minute setup? Tuesday or Wednesday?&quot; | Scheduling |

**Key: Guarantee before price, every time.**

## Part 3: The Sales Angle

### Cold Call Opening (10/10 agents agree: lead with missed calls)

&gt; &quot;Hey [Name], quick question &mdash; you guys do renovation work in [city], right? I built a system that texts back missed calls in 5 seconds. I&apos;m working with a few contractors in [city]. You good for 90 seconds?&quot;

**Why this works:**
- Leads with #1 pain immediately
- No pitch language
- &quot;90 seconds&quot; is specific and respectful
- What it does in one sentence &mdash; no mystery, no AI jargon

### Cold Text (send at 6:30 AM)

&gt; &quot;Hey [Name] &mdash; saw your renovation work on [Google/Kijiji]. Do you ever lose jobs because you couldn&apos;t answer your phone on a job site? I built something that texts back missed calls in 5 seconds. Worth a quick call? &mdash; [Your name]&quot;

### Facebook Group Post

&gt; &quot;Question for the renovation guys here &mdash; how do you handle calls when you&apos;re in the middle of a job? I&apos;ve been talking to contractors around Alberta and it sounds like missing calls is costing people 2-3 jobs a month without even realizing it. Built something that sends an automatic text back in 5 seconds when you miss a call. Not doing a sales pitch &mdash; if anyone wants to see what it looks like, I&apos;ll set up a demo. First few I do I&apos;m not charging until you see results.&quot;

### The Line Nobody Uses (Agent 4)

&gt; &quot;You built the estimate. You shouldn&apos;t have to beg for the job.&quot;

This names the dignity issue directly and stops contractors cold.

## Part 4: The Onboarding Call (30-45 Minutes)

### The Sequence: Pain Resolution Speed

**Pre-Call (Operator, 15 min before):**
- Create client, provision local Twilio number
- Pre-fill KB from Google listing
- Prepare Stripe link
- Have their quote list request ready

---

**Minute 0-3: Anchor in Their Words**

&gt; &quot;You mentioned you lose jobs when you can&apos;t pick up. Let&apos;s make sure that stops today before we hang up.&quot;

Do not do a product tour. Solve Pain 1 first.

---

**Minute 3-8: PAIN 1 &mdash; Missed Call Text-Back (LIVE)**

1. Set up *72 forwarding from their number to the Twilio number
2. Have them call their own number from another phone
3. They watch a text arrive in 5 seconds
4. Say nothing for 4 seconds. Let them sit with it.

**Time to value: 8 minutes into the call.**

&gt; &quot;That&apos;s what happened to the $40K call you missed on Tuesday while you were on the roof.&quot;

---

**Minute 8-12: PAYMENT CAPTURE (Emotional Peak)**

&gt; &quot;Let me get the billing out of the way. I&apos;m texting you a link right now &mdash; first month completely free. Card is just so billing starts automatically on [date]. Cancel anytime.&quot;

Stay on the line. Confirm in Stripe. **Do not proceed to setup before payment.**

---

**Minute 12-22: PAIN 2 &mdash; Dead Quote Reactivation**

&gt; &quot;Now &mdash; those old quotes you never heard back from. How many from the last 6 months?&quot;

Take 3-5 names and numbers. Build the first reactivation message live:

&gt; &quot;Hey [Name], it&apos;s [Contractor] &mdash; you got a quote from us a few months back. Still thinking about the project? Happy to revisit if the timing works.&quot;

Schedule it to go out that afternoon. Tell them:

&gt; &quot;You&apos;re going to see replies within 24 hours, probably sooner.&quot;

**Time to value: 24-48 hours.**

---

**Minute 22-28: KB + Voice Setup**

5 questions, you type while they talk:
1. &quot;What are your top 3 job types?&quot;
2. &quot;What area do you cover?&quot;
3. &quot;When someone asks about price, what do you want the AI to say?&quot;
4. &quot;What do you NOT do?&quot;
5. &quot;What makes you different from the next contractor?&quot;

Read back the AI persona summary. Get verbal approval.

**Critical (Agent 4):** The messages must sound like THEM. &quot;Show me the exact messages before they go out.&quot; This isn&apos;t a feature request &mdash; it&apos;s identity protection.

---

**Minute 28-32: Expectations + Day 7 Booked**

&gt; &quot;Week 1: missed call text-back is live, old quotes are getting followed up. Week 2: AI handles new inquiries with a review window. Week 3: fully automatic. I check in every 2 weeks with a report showing exactly what happened.&quot;

Book the Day 7 check-in BEFORE hanging up.

---

**Minute 32-35: Agency Trauma Neutralizer**

&gt; &quot;Last thing &mdash; this is month-to-month. No contract. You can cancel by texting me. The only reason you&apos;d stay is because it&apos;s working. Every two weeks you get a report your wife can look at. If the math doesn&apos;t work, we stop.&quot;

---

### Post-Call (Operator, Same Day, 30 min)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Complete KB entries from call notes |
| 2 | Fire outbound reactivation batch to dead quotes |
| 3 | Verify *72 forwarding with a test call |
| 4 | Send recap SMS: &quot;You&apos;re live. Send me those old quotes.&quot; |
| 5 | Revenue Leak Audit within 48 hours |

Each win raises the floor and reveals the next gap. The product doesn&apos;t need to be &quot;sold&quot; after Day 3 &mdash; it just needs to keep working.

## Part 6: The Referral Story

### The Story at the Supplier House (10/10 agents agree)

&gt; &quot;I was on a job site, missed a call, and 5 seconds later the system had already texted the homeowner. But here&apos;s the crazy part &mdash; he also found 12 old quotes in my phone from last year. Sent them a message. Three replied. One of those is a $40K kitchen. I paid five grand last year to a marketing company and got nothing. This costs me a thousand a month and it&apos;s already paid for itself twice over. Guy&apos;s name is [Name], I&apos;ll text you his number.&quot;

### Why This Story Spreads:
1. **Specific dollar amount** ($40K kitchen) &mdash; other contractors run the math
2. **Competitor contrast** (&quot;five grand for nothing vs a thousand that works&quot;)
3. **Zero effort narrative** (&quot;I didn&apos;t do anything&quot;)
4. **No jargon** &mdash; no AI, no automation, no platform
5. **Ends with a referral action** (&quot;I&apos;ll text you his number&quot;)

### The Referral Trigger (Agent 3):
Contractors don&apos;t refer because the product is good. They refer because **telling the story makes them look like a savvy business operator** &mdash; not just a guy who swings a hammer. The reward isn&apos;t money. It&apos;s the identity of being the guy who figured it out first.

### The 5 Conditions for a Referral (Agent 8):
1. They have a specific story with a dollar amount
2. They feel like an early adopter, not a late follower
3. No fear of embarrassment if it doesn&apos;t work for their peer
4. The referral benefits them socially, not just financially
5. They have been explicitly asked, at the right moment (after a visible win)

## Part 7: Critical Failure Cascade to Prevent

### The Worst Thing That Can Happen (Agent 10, 8/10 agents agree):

The AI sends a message to someone the contractor knows personally &mdash; a friend, family member, or long-term client. The message is tone-deaf to that context.

**Example:** Contractor has a 10-year friend who asked for a quote as a favor. The AI sends &quot;following up on your quote &mdash; still interested?&quot; Friend texts the contractor: &quot;Did you just have a bot text me? That&apos;s weird.&quot;

**The cascade:** Embarrassment &rarr; distrust of ALL system messages &rarr; wants to approve every message manually &rarr; eliminates the value proposition &rarr; churns with the story &quot;it texted my buddy and made me look like an idiot.&quot;

**Mandatory mitigation:** Onboarding MUST include an exclusion list step:

&gt; &quot;Before we run anything &mdash; are there any contacts you want us to skip? Family, close friends, anyone you have a personal relationship with?&quot;

This cannot be optional. Frame it as protecting their relationships, not a product limitation.

You&apos;re not selling a feature. You&apos;re selling an identity upgrade.

&gt; **&quot;I make more money with it than without it, and I don&apos;t have to do anything different.&quot;**

Everything &mdash; onboarding, value stacking, proof points, referral strategy &mdash; must serve that sentence.

## Divergences

### Lead with Missed Calls vs Dead Quotes on the Cold Call

**Missed calls (7/10):** Visceral, daily, specific memory. Every contractor has THE story. Objections are defensive but the demo resolves them.

**Dead quotes (3/10, Agents 4, 8, 10):** Objections are collaborative (&quot;how?&quot;) not defensive (&quot;why?&quot;). Sets correct product expectations. But harder to demo live on a cold call.

**Resolution:** Lead with missed calls to OPEN the conversation. Pivot to dead quotes to CLOSE the deal. &quot;We solve the missed calls automatically. But the bigger win is the 20 quotes sitting in your phone right now that went silent.&quot;

### Call Duration: 30 min vs 45 min

**30 minutes (4/10):** Tight, respects contractor&apos;s time. Requires pre-call prep.

**45 minutes (6/10):** More realistic for first clients with no established process.

**Resolution:** Plan for 45, aim for 30. The pre-call prep determines which you get.
