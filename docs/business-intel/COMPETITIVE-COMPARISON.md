## When Each Alternative Is Actually Better

Be brutally honest about this:

### GHL Agency
**Better when:** The contractor also needs lead generation (Google Ads, SEO, landing pages) from a single vendor.

**Why:** ConversionSurgery does not generate new leads — it recovers existing ones. If the contractor is starting from zero inbound pipeline, GHL's all-in-one platform may be the right first move. Once they have inbound volume, CS is the next layer.

---

### Freelance VA
**Better when:** The contractor needs an operational assistant for the full business (permits, suppliers, subcontractor coordination), not just lead follow-up. Also better for $2M+ contractors with high-touch, relationship-driven sales on $100K+ projects where the VA becomes a trusted business partner.

**Why:** A good VA brings business judgment and can handle exceptions. CS is automation only — it cannot negotiate scope or handle truly complex deals. A VA can also work 10–15 hours/week across the whole business, not just leads.

---

### Answering Service
**Better when:** The contractor's leads are 80%+ inbound phone calls from a 55+ demographic that will not engage via SMS or web.

**Why:** For voice-only businesses, an answering service is proven, reliable, and the contractor doesn't learn a new tool. CS requires texting willingness in the prospect pool.

---

### Jobber/ServiceTitan
**Not a direct competitor.** It's a job management and invoicing tool.

**Right answer for most contractors:** CS + Jobber, not one or the other. Jobber handles the job; CS handles the lead. They integrate well.

---

### Doing Nothing
**Only rational for:** Contractors at full capacity who are turning away work and have no interest in growing.
