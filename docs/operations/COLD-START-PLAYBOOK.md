# Cold-Start Playbook: First Client in 21 Days

Zero network. Zero clients. Zero social proof. Here is the exact plan.

---

## The Critical Path

**3 channels, run simultaneously. Combined probability of closing client #1 in 21 days: 75-85%.**

| Channel | Why | Reply Rate | Close Rate | P(1 client, 21 days) |
|---------|-----|:----------:|:----------:|:-------------------:|
| **Cold calling** (volume engine) | Contractors answer their phones. Demo is phone-native. | 25-40% pickup | 20-30% | 35-55% |
| **Facebook Groups** (warm pool) | Contractors self-identify their pain. You DM the engaged ones. | 15-35% on DMs | 15-25% | 20-35% |
| **Kijiji/FB Marketplace replies** (warm intent) | They already posted an ad &mdash; they want work. | 20-35% | 15-25% | 70-80% |

Running all three: **75-85% probability** in 21 days.

1. Send 20 Kijiji/FB Marketplace replies (see Script A below)
2. Post in 2 Facebook Groups (see Script B below)
3. Make 10 cold calls from Google Maps list (see Script C below)
4. Send 5 text messages to contractors with visible phone numbers (see Script D below)

### Day 2-5

Continue the same cadence:
- 30-40 cold calls/day
- 10-15 DMs/texts per day

### Day 6-7 (Weekend)

Rest. Respond to messages only. Do not cold call on weekends.

## The Scripts

### Script A: Kijiji / FB Marketplace Reply

> Hi [Name] &mdash; saw your ad for renovation work in [city].
>
> Quick question: when you send out an estimate and the homeowner goes quiet, do you have anything that follows up automatically, or does it just die?
>
> I run a follow-up service for Alberta renovation contractors. We send automated texts after your estimates so you close more of the jobs you already quoted. Most contractors are losing 2-3 jobs a month just from no follow-up.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call to see if it fits?

---

### Script B: Facebook Group Post (NOT a pitch)

> Quick question for Alberta contractors &mdash; when a lead goes quiet after you send a quote, what do you actually do? Do you follow up, and if so, how many times before you write it off?
>
> Asking because I&apos;ve been talking to a lot of contractors and the answer varies wildly. Some follow up once, some never follow up. Curious what works here.

**Do NOT mention your product.** DM people who comment:

> Hey [Name] &mdash; saw your comment in the group. Sounds like this is a real headache for you too. I actually built something that handles exactly this &mdash; mind if I show you? Takes 30 seconds.

---

### Script C: Cold Call

**When they pick up:**

> Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] &mdash; I&apos;ll be super quick, 60 seconds. I built a tool that texts back missed calls in 5 seconds &mdash; every missed call, automatically. I know you guys miss calls on job sites all the time. I&apos;m not pitching you today. Can I send you our demo number right now? You call it, hang up, and your phone buzzes in 5 seconds. That&apos;s it.

**If busy:** &ldquo;I figured &mdash; that&apos;s literally why I&apos;m calling. I&apos;ll text you the number right now. Try it when you&apos;re done today.&rdquo;

**Voicemail:**

> Hey [Name], this is [Your Name]. I built something for renovation contractors &mdash; texts back missed calls in 5 seconds, automatically. I know that&apos;s a problem when you&apos;re on site. My number&apos;s [your number]. I&apos;ll try you again [day].

---

### Script D: Cold Text (send at 7:00 AM)

> Hey [Name] &mdash; saw you do renos in [area]. Honest question: when a lead goes quiet after the quote, do you have anything that follows up automatically, or does it just die?
>
> Not a sales pitch yet. Genuinely asking.
> &mdash; [Your Name]

**The single most effective opening line (from the contractor-empathy agent):**

> Do you know which of your quoted jobs from the last 90 days you actually lost, or do they just go quiet and you never find out?

>

---

### Script F: Demo Call (15 minutes)

**Opening (2 min):**

> Before I show you anything &mdash; tell me: in the last month, how many estimates did you send that went quiet?

Let them answer. Then:

> And if even one of those came back as a yes, what&apos;s that worth roughly?

They do the math for you. A kitchen is $35-65K.

**Demo (8 min):**

> Let me show you what happens when those leads go through the system instead.

Show the demo. Walk through one lead cycle. Show what the homeowner sees. Show what the contractor sees.

**Close (5 min):**

> Here&apos;s what I propose: first month free. I set everything up. You pay nothing until you see a result. If you recover one job, it pays for itself 20 times over. If you don&apos;t see value in 30 days, you owe nothing.
>
> The only thing I ask: if it works, introduce me to two other contractors you know.
>
> Can I get you set up this week?

---

### Script G: Follow-Up (3 days, no reply)

> Hey [Name] &mdash; following up from [day]. Totally fine if the timing&apos;s off.
>
> Short version: I built a follow-up system for renovation contractors. First month free. If it&apos;s not something you need, just say so and I won&apos;t bother you again.
>
> Worth 15 minutes?

**Second follow-up (5 days later, still no reply):**

> Last message from me on this. If following up with dead quotes isn&apos;t a problem for you, completely understand. If it is, happy to show you what I built. Either way, hope the work&apos;s going well.

Do not send a third follow-up to cold prospects.

---

### Script H: &ldquo;I Need to Think About It&rdquo;

> Totally fair. Can I ask what specifically is giving you pause? Is it the cost, the tech side, or something else?

Handle the specific objection. Then:

> Here&apos;s what I&apos;d suggest: don&apos;t decide today. Let me get you set up on the free month &mdash; takes 20 minutes. You use it for two weeks. If nothing happens, you cancel and owe nothing. The only way to know is to run it.

If still no:

> No problem. Can I check back [specific day]? Does mornings or afternoons work better?

Lock a specific date and time. Call at exactly that time.

## What Will Kill This

1. **Spending Day 1-3 &ldquo;perfecting the message&rdquo;** &mdash; the scripts above are ready. Send them.
2. **Generic openers** &mdash; &ldquo;I help contractors grow&rdquo; gets ignored. Reference their specific work/city/post.
3. **Pitching before qualifying** &mdash; the opener must end with a question, not a link.
4. **Skipping follow-ups** &mdash; 70% of closes happen on follow-up #2 or #3.
5. **Dropping volume below 8 contacts/day** &mdash; the math stops working.
