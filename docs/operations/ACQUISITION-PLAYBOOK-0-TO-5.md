# 0 to 5 Clients: Acquisition Playbook

Target: $5,000/month (5 clients × $1,000/month) Timeline: 30 days to first client, 90 days to five Stage: Pre-first-client — everything here is manual, low-cost, high-touch

---

## The Core Idea

You already have the outreach asset most agencies dream about: the Revenue Leak Audit. Your playbook already calls for delivering one within 48 hours of onboarding. The move is to deliver it *before* they're a client — as the cold outreach itself. It's personalized, it uses their own data, and it positions you as someone who does the work, not someone who sends pitch decks.

The "call your own number" live demo is your closer. The audit opens the door. The demo shuts it.

---

## Phase 0: Setup (Days 1–3)

### Pick your starting city

**Calgary** — larger renovation market, more contractors in the $500K–$3M ICP range, higher competition (which means contractors feel the pain of slow follow-up more). Concentrate here until you have 3+ clients. One city builds pattern recognition fast — by prospect #10 your audits take half the time because you know the local market.

### Build your prospect list

You need 30 qualified contractors. Here's where to find them and what to look for.

**Source 1: Google Maps (primary)**

Search these terms in Calgary:

- "kitchen renovation Calgary"
- "basement renovation Calgary"
- "bathroom renovation Calgary"
- "home renovation contractor Calgary"
- "general contractor Calgary"

For each result, capture:

- Business name
- Google review count + star rating
- Whether they respond to Google reviews (scroll through a few)
- Website URL
- Phone number (from GBP listing)
- Email (from website)
- Whether their website has a contact form
- Whether they're running Google Ads (look for "Sponsored" tag in search results)

**Source 2: HomeStars / Houzz / BBB**

Cross-reference your Google Maps list. Contractors active on multiple platforms are more likely to care about their online presence — and more likely to have follow-up gaps because they're getting leads from multiple channels they can't keep up with.

**Source 3: Facebook Groups**

Search "Calgary renovation" or "Calgary contractors" on Facebook. Many Alberta contractors are active in local trade groups. This also gives you a DM channel for outreach.

### Qualification filter

Score each prospect. You want contractors who meet at least 3 of these 5:

| Signal | What it tells you | How to check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 30–200 Google reviews | Active business, cares about reputation | Google Business Profile |
| Has a website with contact form | Getting web leads they need to respond to | Visit their site |
| Running Google Ads | Paying for leads — makes your ROI pitch concrete | Search their trade + "Calgary" |
| Hasn't responded to recent Google reviews | Follow-up gap is visible | Scroll their reviews |
| 2–10 person team (not a solo operator, not a large firm) | Right size for $1K/month to make sense | Website "About" page, team photos |

**Disqualify if:**

- Solo operator with &lt;10 reviews (too small, $1K is a stretch)
- Large firm with 50+ employees (they have office staff already)
- No web presence at all (they won't value what you do)
- Already using a CRM/answering service (visible on their site — "powered by" footers, chat widgets)

### Rank your list

Put your top 10 at the top — these are the ones with the clearest gaps and the most visible pain. You'll audit these first.

### Set up your demo line

Before any outreach, have your platform's missed-call text-back working on a demo number. You need to be able to say "call this number, let it ring, watch what happens" on every sales call. Test it yourself 5 times. Make sure it fires in under 5 seconds every time.

## Phase 1: Outreach (Days 4–14)

### The outreach asset: Cold Revenue Leak Audit

For each of your top 10 prospects, produce a shortened version of the Revenue Leak Audit. This is NOT the full 48-hour onboarding audit — it's a 15-minute version using only publicly available data. Keep it to one page.

**What to include:**

1. **Their Google review snapshot** — review count, star rating, and how they compare to their top 3 local competitors. If competitors have 2–3× more reviews, say so.

2. **Response test result** — submit their contact form (or call and let it ring). Note how long it took to get a response. If it took more than 30 minutes, that's your headline finding. If they never responded, even better (for your pitch).

3. **Review response gap** — if their last 10 Google reviews have zero owner responses, note it. Homeowners notice this.

4. **One revenue estimate** — conservative. "Based on your review volume and the typical close rate in residential renovation, each month of delayed follow-up likely costs $2,000–5,000 in lost projects." Use the low end. Undersell this number.

**Format:** Plain text email or a clean one-page PDF. Don't over-design it. The value is in the data, not the presentation.

**Time per audit:** 15–20 minutes once you know the drill. First few will take 30.

### The outreach message

Subject line: "\[Business Name\] — found something you should see"

Body (email version):

> \[First name\],
>
> I put together a quick revenue audit for \[Business Name\] — no strings attached, just thought you'd find it useful.
>
> The short version: \[one specific finding — e.g., "I submitted your contact form on Tuesday at 2 PM and haven't heard back yet. Your competitor \[name\] responded in 4 minutes."\]
>
> The full breakdown is attached — takes 2 minutes to read.
>
> If any of it resonates, I can show you something live in 15 minutes that most contractors find pretty eye-opening. No pitch deck, no slides — I'll just have you call a number and watch what happens.
>
> Either way, the audit is yours to keep.
>
> Mashrur ConversionSurgery

### Channel strategy (multi-touch, same asset)

Send the audit through every channel you can find for each contractor. You don't need all of them to land — you need one.

| Channel | When to use | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Email (business) | Always — this is your primary | Most contractor websites list an email or have a contact form |
| Email (personal) | If findable via LinkedIn or social | Don't be creepy about it — only if it's publicly listed |
| Facebook DM | If they have a business page | Many Alberta contractors check FB more than email |
| Instagram DM | If they're active on IG | Renovation contractors post project photos — check |
| LinkedIn | If the owner has a profile | Less common for trades, but some have it |
| Google Business Profile message | If messaging is enabled on their GBP | Easy to check — look for "Message" button on their listing |
| Website contact form | Always — use their own form | Short version: "I put together a revenue audit for \[Business Name\]. Where should I send it?" |

**Timing:** Send the initial outreach Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM. Contractors check messages before heading to job sites.

**Volume:** 2 audits per day, 5 days per week = 10 per week. Don't batch more than this — quality drops and you burn out.

### Follow-up cadence

| Day | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Day 0 | Send audit via email + one other channel |
| Day 3 | Follow-up on the channel that's most likely to be seen (FB/text if no email open) — "Just checking if you saw the audit I sent over. Happy to walk through it in 10 minutes if useful." |
| Day 7 | Final follow-up — different angle: "No worries if the timing isn't right. One thing I'd suggest regardless — \[one actionable tip from their audit, e.g., 'responding to your Google reviews would help, your competitors are doing it'\]. That alone is worth doing." |
| Day 7+ | Move to nurture list (monthly check-in). Do not follow up again for 30 days. |

Three touches max. No more. You're not a pest — you're the person who did free work for them. If they don't respond to three touches with a personalized audit, the timing is wrong, not the pitch.

## Phase 2: Convert (Days 7–30)

### When they respond

Most responses will be one of three types:

**"This is interesting, tell me more"**→ Book a 15-minute call. Not 30, not an hour. 15 minutes. Frame it as: "I'll show you something live — it takes 2 minutes — and then we can talk about whether it makes sense for your business."

**"How much does it cost?"**→ Don't answer in text. Say: "It's $1,000/month, month-to-month, no contract. But before we talk pricing I want to show you something — it takes 2 minutes on a call and it'll tell you more than any pricing page. When's good for 15 minutes this week?"

**"I'm not interested right now"**→ "No problem at all. The audit is yours to keep. If anything changes, I'm here." Add to nurture list.

### The 15-minute demo call

This is your entire sales process. It's not a discovery call. It's not a consultation. It's a demo with one killer moment.

**Structure:**

1. **(2 min) Acknowledge the audit finding.** "You saw in the audit that \[specific finding\]. That's actually pretty common — most renovation contractors in Calgary have the same gap. The question is what happens when a lead texts you at 6 PM on a Thursday and you're mid-demo."

2. **(2 min) Set up the demo.** "I want to show you something. Can you call this number on your phone right now? \[Give demo number.\] Let it ring — don't leave a voicemail — just hang up after 3-4 rings."

3. **(30 sec) Let them experience it.** They call. They hang up. Within 5 seconds, a text arrives on their phone: "Hey, sorry we missed your call! What can we help you with?" Say nothing. Let them react.

4. **(3 min) Explain what just happened.** "That text went out in under 5 seconds. If that was a homeowner looking for a kitchen quote, you just captured them before they called the next contractor on Google. Now imagine that happening for every missed call, every form fill, every text — and then the AI follows up on every estimate you send for 6 weeks straight."

5. **(3 min) The pitch.** "It's $1,000/month. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. No setup fee. We run the whole thing for you — your only job is to keep doing great work. The system responds to leads, follows up on estimates, books appointments into your Google Calendar, collects payments, and asks for reviews. You spend about 15 minutes a week on it. If after 30 days you haven't seen 5 real lead conversations, your first month is free. If after 90 days you can't point to a single project it helped you win, we refund your last month and you keep every lead."

6. **(3 min) Handle objections.** Use the quick-reference from your playbook Section 12. The three most common at this stage:

   - "What if the AI says something wrong?" → Smart Assist review window in Week 2, guardrails, escalation.
   - "$1,000 is a lot." → One kitchen project pays for the year. The risk is one month.
   - "I need to think about it." → "Totally fair. The audit is yours regardless. But every week without this, the gap it describes keeps costing you."

7. **Close or schedule next step.** Either they say yes and you schedule the onboarding call, or they need time. If they need time, send the post-call follow-up email from your playbook within 2 hours.

### First client onboarding

When you close your first client, follow Section 10 of your Managed Service Playbook to the letter. But add one thing: **ask for a referral at Day 30.**

Not before. Not during onboarding. At Day 30, after the first bi-weekly report, after they've seen the system work, text them:

> "Hey \[name\] — glad the system's been working for you. Do you know any other contractors who might be dealing with the same follow-up gap? Happy to put together a free audit for them like I did for you."

Referrals from active clients close at 3–5× the rate of cold outreach. One happy contractor in Calgary's renovation community is worth 10 cold audits.

## Phase 3: Scale to 5 (Days 30–90)

### The math

- 10 audits/week × 4 weeks = 40 contractors contacted in Month 1
- Realistic response rate on personalized audits: 15–25%
- Of those who respond, realistic demo-to-close rate: 20–30%
- Expected clients from 40 contacts: 1–3

That's Month 1. To hit 5 by Month 3:

- Month 1: 40 audits → 1–3 clients
- Month 2: 40 audits + referrals from Month 1 clients → 1–3 more
- Month 3: 20 audits + referrals → fill remaining spots

This is conservative. The audit-based outreach will likely outperform generic cold email by a wide margin because it's genuinely useful even if they never buy.

### What changes after client #1

1. **You have a case study.** Even if it's just "we responded to 47 leads in 30 days with an average response time of 8 seconds for \[trade type\] contractor in Calgary." That's real.

2. **Your audits get faster.** You know the Calgary market now. You know the competitor landscape. Each audit drops from 20 minutes to 10.

3. **Referrals start.** The Day 30 referral ask is your lowest-cost acquisition channel.

4. **Your demo gets better.** You're no longer saying "imagine this working for a contractor." You're saying "here's what happened for \[first client\] in their first month."

### Nurture list

Every contractor who didn't close goes on a monthly nurture list. Once a month, send them one useful thing — not a pitch:

- "3 Calgary contractors got hit with negative Google reviews this week. Here's the best response template I've seen."
- "HomeStars just changed their ranking algorithm. Here's what it means for renovation contractors."
- "I audited 30 Calgary renovation contractors this month. Average form response time: 6 hours. Fastest: 12 seconds."

Position yourself as the person who understands their market. When they're ready, they'll remember you.

## What Not to Do

1. **Don't build an automated outreach system.** Manual for the first 5. You'll learn what resonates and what doesn't. Automation locks in bad assumptions.

2. **Don't spend money on ads.** Your conversion happens in the demo, not on a landing page. Ads bring people to landing pages. You need people on calls.

3. **Don't pitch via text/email.** The audit opens the conversation. The demo closes it. Never try to sell the service in writing — it's too complex and the "call your number" moment can't happen in an email.

4. **Don't discount.** $1,000/month with month-to-month and a guarantee is already the lowest-risk version of this offer. If someone wants a discount, they're not your ICP — move on.

5. **Don't try to close on the first touch.** The audit is the first touch. It's not a pitch. It's a gift. The pitch happens on the demo call, if they want one.

6. **Don't expand beyond Calgary before 5 clients.** Depth beats breadth at this stage.

## Tracking

Keep it dead simple. A Google Sheet with these columns:

| Column | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Business name | Who |
| Contact name | Who specifically |
| Trade | Kitchen, basement, general, etc. |
| Google reviews | Quick-reference for priority |
| Audit sent date | When you sent the outreach |
| Channels used | Email, FB, IG, etc. |
| Response? | Yes/No/Date |
| Demo scheduled? | Date or N/A |
| Demo result | Closed / Follow-up / Not interested |
| Status | Active prospect / Nurture / Client / Dead |

Don't overcomplicate this. Don't build a CRM for yourself. A spreadsheet with 30 rows is all you need until you have 5 paying clients.

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Check weekly |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Audits sent | 10/week | Friday afternoon |
| Response rate | &gt;15% | After each batch of 10 |
| Demo calls booked | 2–3/week | Ongoing |
| Close rate | &gt;20% of demos | Monthly |
| Days to first client | &lt;30 | — |
| Days to five clients | &lt;90 | — |

If your response rate is below 10% after 20 audits, the audit content needs work — not the volume. If demos aren't converting, record yourself (with permission) and review the demo flow.
