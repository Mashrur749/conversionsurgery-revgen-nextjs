## Section 1 — Speed-to-Lead Assessment

**What to research:**
Call or text the business number (use a personal number, not a ConversionSurgery number). Time how long it takes to receive a response. Note exactly what happens: voicemail, no answer, live pickup, auto-reply.

- If voicemail: did it pick up immediately or ring through? Was there a callback prompt?
- If no answer: try twice at different times within a business-hours window.
- If live pickup: the contractor has staff handling calls. Adjust the audit — the speed-to-lead finding is less urgent; pivot the narrative to follow-up depth instead.

**Research notes (internal only):**
- Time of first call attempt: ___________
- Result: ( ) No answer ( ) Voicemail ( ) Live pickup ( ) Auto-reply
- Time of response received (if any): ___________
- Response type: ___________

**Finding (include in audit):**

> [Business Name] responds to new inquiries in approximately [X hours / X minutes] on average. [OR: No response was received after 24 hours of an initial call attempt during business hours.]

## Section 2 — Missed Call Analysis

**What to research:**
Ask the contractor during the onboarding call: "Roughly what percentage of calls do you think go to voicemail when you are on a job?" Most will estimate 30–60%. If they have a Google Business Profile with call history enabled, check the Calls tab in Google Business Profile Manager for weekly missed call data. Accept the contractor's estimate if GBP data is unavailable.

**Research notes (internal only):**

- GBP call history available: ( ) Yes ( ) No
- Weekly missed calls from GBP (if available): ___________

**Finding (include in audit):**

> An estimated [X]% of inbound calls during business hours go unanswered, either to voicemail or with no response. Each missed call is a potential job that does not get a quote.

- If they have not sent a list: use "X+" with a note that the full list is pending.
- Conservative recovery rate: assume 10–15% of dormant quotes respond and close.

- Average job value (from onboarding call): $___________
- Total pipeline value (count x avg): $___________

## Section 4 — Review Gap

**What to research:**
Search "[Business Name]" on Google Maps. Note their current review count and average rating. Then search "[trade] [city]" (e.g., "roofing contractor Atlanta") and identify the top 3 results. Note their review counts. Calculate the average of those 3 competitors.

- If the contractor is already the top result: shift the finding to review velocity (are they falling behind? Are competitors adding reviews faster?).
- Do not fabricate competitor data. If you cannot find clear competitors, note that clearly.

**Research notes (internal only):**
- [Business Name] current reviews: ___________ ( ___star avg)
- Competitor 1 — name: ___________ reviews: ___________ stars: ___
- Competitor 2 — name: ___________ reviews: ___________ stars: ___
- Competitor 3 — name: ___________ reviews: ___________ stars: ___
- Competitor average review count: ___________

**Finding (include in audit):**

> [Business Name] currently has [X] Google reviews, while the top 3 local competitors average [Y] reviews. Closing this gap improves both search ranking and conversion rate from profile visits to calls.

Common answers: nothing, a personal text if they remember, Jobber reminders, or HubSpot sequences. Note the tool if mentioned — it helps position ConversionSurgery as a complement, not a replacement.

**Research notes (internal only):**

## Section 6 — Revenue Impact Estimate

**What to calculate:**
Use the data from Sections 3–5 to build a conservative one-paragraph ROI statement. Pull one specific project type the contractor mentioned during the onboarding call (e.g., kitchen remodel, roof replacement, HVAC install) and anchor the math to a real number.

Formula:
- Monthly service fee: $1,000
- One recovered project value: $[avg job value from Section 3]
- Months of service covered by one project: $[job value] / $1,000

Keep the math conservative. Do not imply certainty. Use "could" and "approximately."

**Research notes (internal only):**
- Service fee per month: $1,000
- Average job value used: $___________
- Months covered by one project: ___________
- Specific project type to reference: ___________

**Finding (include in audit):**

> Recovering even 1 of the [X] outstanding quotes at [Business Name]'s typical [project type] value of approximately $[job value] would cover approximately [X] months of service. The goal in Week 1 is to contact every dormant quote before a competitor does.
