# CS + Your Tool — Integration Objection Handler

**Who uses this:** Sales reps, before or during a call with a prospect who already uses an FSM or CRM tool.

**The question this answers:** "Does it integrate with [Jobber / Housecall Pro / ServiceTitan / Buildertrend / etc.]?"

---

## The Core Positioning (memorize this)

ConversionSurgery works the **front** of the funnel — from first contact through booked appointment.

Your existing tool works the **back** — project management, dispatch, invoicing, job tracking.

They do not compete. They hand off.

> "Think of it like a front desk and a job site supervisor. The front desk catches the call, qualifies the lead, and gets them on the calendar. The supervisor takes it from there. You wouldn't ask your job site supervisor to answer the phone — same idea."

---

## Per-Tool Scripts

### Jobber

> "Great news — we integrate with Jobber. When we book an appointment, Jobber gets notified. When you complete a job in Jobber, we automatically request a review. You keep using Jobber for everything you do now."

**Notes for rep:** Jobber integration exists. Lead this with confidence.

---

### Housecall Pro

> "We do not currently integrate directly with Housecall Pro. The platform works alongside it &mdash; contractor manages their schedule in Housecall Pro, ConversionSurgery handles lead capture and follow-up. When we book an appointment, you create the job in Housecall Pro. When you finish the job, let us know and we&apos;ll request a review."

**Notes for rep:** No native integration &mdash; be upfront. The handoff is one step (create the job). Most HCP users are already doing this manually anyway. This is not a blocker.

---

### ServiceTitan

> "ServiceTitan is great for dispatch and job management. We fill a different gap — instant lead response and estimate follow-up. ServiceTitan doesn&apos;t text back a missed call in 5 seconds or follow up on your estimates for 14 days. We do that, then hand the qualified lead to you for ServiceTitan."

**Notes for rep:** ServiceTitan users are usually larger operations and more skeptical. Emphasize the speed-to-lead gap — ServiceTitan explicitly does not solve this. The 5-second callback and 14-day estimate follow-up are differentiators ServiceTitan has no answer for.

---

### Buildertrend / CoConstruct / Other Project Management Tools

> "Your project management tool handles everything after the job is sold. We handle everything before — catching the lead, responding instantly, following up on the estimate, and booking the appointment. You keep using [tool] exactly as you do now."

**Notes for rep:** These tools are built for remodelers and builders who manage long projects. Zero lead response functionality. The gap is obvious once you point it out. Substitute the actual tool name in the script.

---

### QuickBooks Only

> "QuickBooks handles your money. We handle your leads. No overlap. When a job closes, our payment reminder links work alongside your QuickBooks invoicing — or we can turn off our payment reminders if you prefer to keep everything in QuickBooks."

**Notes for rep:** QuickBooks-only contractors are usually small operators. They appreciate simplicity. Lead with the zero-overlap framing. If they push back on duplicate invoicing, offer to disable CS payment reminders (see configuration section below).

## The "Two Systems" Objection

**Prospect says:** "I don&apos;t want to manage two systems."

**Response:**

> "I get it — nobody wants to manage two systems. Here&apos;s the thing: you don&apos;t manage ours. We manage it for you. Your only interaction is reading a Monday text with your pipeline numbers and texting WON or LOST when a job closes. Everything else — lead response, follow-up, booking, reviews — runs without you touching anything."

**If they push further:**

> "What does managing it look like day-to-day? You get a text Monday morning. You reply WON or LOST to two or three jobs. That&apos;s it. The system handles the rest."

## What to Configure for FSM Users

When onboarding a contractor who uses an FSM (Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, Jobber, etc.), review these settings with them:

| Feature | Default | FSM User Recommendation | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| Payment reminders | ON | **DISABLE** if they invoice through their FSM | Avoids duplicate requests to the homeowner |
| Review requests | ON | **KEEP ON** | Most FSMs do not follow up on reviews reliably — this is a CS advantage |
| Estimate follow-up | ON | **KEEP ON** | No FSM provides this — it is the core value prop |
| Lead response (AI) | ON | **KEEP ON** | No FSM does instant AI response — this is why they are talking to you |

**Rule of thumb:** Disable anything that creates duplicate homeowner contact. Keep everything that fills a gap the FSM does not cover.

## Quick Reference Card (phone-friendly)

**Jobber** — integrated, lead with confidence.

**Housecall Pro** — no direct integration, manual one-step handoff, not a blocker.

**ServiceTitan** — no overlap, different jobs, emphasize speed-to-lead gap.

**Buildertrend / CoConstruct** — PM tools, zero lead response, obvious gap.

**QuickBooks** — money vs. leads, zero overlap, offer to disable payment reminders if needed.

**Two systems objection** — "You don&apos;t manage ours. Monday text + WON/LOST replies. That&apos;s it."
