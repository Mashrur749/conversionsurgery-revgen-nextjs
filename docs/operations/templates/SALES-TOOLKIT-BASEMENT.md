## SECTION 1: COLD CALL SCRIPT WITH GUARDRAILS

### The QVC Framework

Every call follows three beats: **Question** (open with curiosity, not a pitch), **Value** (show the outcome in their language), **CTA** (one clear next step).

### Opening (You Have 8 Seconds)

> "Hey, is this [Name]? ... Hey [Name], it's Mashrur calling from Calgary. Quick question -- when you're on a job and your phone buzzes with a new lead, what usually happens to that call?"

Then STOP. Do not say another word. Let them answer.

---

### Branch: They Engage

**If they say "goes to voicemail" / "I try to call back later" / "my wife grabs it sometimes":**

> "Yeah, that's what I hear from every basement guy I talk to. I built something that catches that call, texts the homeowner back in 5 seconds, and books the site visit -- while you're still holding the drill. Want to see how it works? Takes 60 seconds on the phone right now."

If they say yes, go to **Section 3: Instant Demo**.

If they say "yeah tell me more" but don't want the demo yet:

> "So here's the short version. When a homeowner calls and you can't answer, the system picks up, takes their info, answers their questions about your services, and books the estimate visit into your calendar. Then it follows up on every quote you send until you get a yes or a no. You don't manage anything -- I run the whole thing for you. First month is free. Want to try it on your next busy week?"

---

### Branch: "Send Me Something"

> "I will -- what's your best email? And while I have you -- how many estimates went cold on you last month that you never followed up on?"

If they give a number (even "a few"), that's your opening:

> "That's exactly what this thing is built for. Those quotes sitting in your sent folder -- the system follows up on every single one until they give you an answer. Most basement guys tell me 20-30% of those come back. I'll send you the info, but honestly the fastest way to see it is a 2-minute call. Can I grab 15 minutes Saturday morning?"

If they just want the email and want to go:

> "Done. I'll send it over tonight. I'll follow up Monday -- if it's not for you, just tell me and I won't bug you again."

Then send the **Leave-Behind** (Section 5) immediately.

---

### Branch: "Not Interested"

> "Totally fair. Can I ask one thing -- do you have a system for following up on estimates that go quiet?"

If they answer the question, they're interested -- they just don't know it yet. Keep the conversation going naturally.

If they say "yeah we're good" or hang up, move on. No hard feelings.

---

### Branch: "What Does It Cost?"

Always state the guarantee before the price. Every time.

> "First month is free -- you pay nothing until you see it working. After that it's a thousand a month. Month-to-month, no contract, cancel anytime. And one recovered basement job covers the whole year. The real question is: how many quotes are sitting in your phone right now that went quiet?"

---

### Branch: "I'm Too Busy Right Now"

> "That's actually the perfect time -- you're busy, missing calls, leads going to the next guy on Google. When you're slow is when you don't need this. When you're slammed is when it matters most. Can I grab 15 minutes this Saturday morning?"

---

### Branch: Awkward Silence

> "I know this is out of the blue -- I'm just a local guy in Calgary who built this thing specifically for basement contractors. No pressure at all. Is this something worth a quick look, or should I let you get back to it?"

---

### Branch: Clearly Not a Fit

> "Sounds like you've got it covered -- that's great. If anything changes, I'm easy to find. Have a good one, [Name]."

### NOTES FOR MASHRUR (Read These Before Every Call Session)

1. **Take a breath after your opening question.** Do not fill the silence. Let THEM answer. The silence feels longer to you than to them. Count to 3 in your head if you have to.

2. **If they're eating dinner, on a job site, or clearly interrupted** -- apologize and ask when to call back. Do not pitch someone who's annoyed at the interruption. Say: "Sounds like I caught you at a bad time -- when's better to try you?"

3. **It's OK to stumble.** You're a person, not a telemarketer. That's your advantage. If you lose your place, just say: "Sorry, let me back up -- the reason I'm calling is..." Contractors respect real people over polished scripts.

4. **After every call, write down:** What objection came up? What did I say? What worked? What felt awkward? This is how you build YOUR version of the script. By call 20, half of these lines will be yours, not mine.

5. **Your first 5 calls will be rough.** By call 10, you'll have the rhythm. By call 20, you won't need the script. That's normal for everyone who has ever cold-called.

6. **Best times to call contractors:** Tuesday-Thursday, 11:30am-12:30pm (lunch break) or 4:30-5:30pm (wrapping up on site). Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons. Saturday 9-10am is surprisingly good -- they're home, relaxed, thinking about business.

7. **Never say "AI" or "automated."** Say "the system" or "your response team." Never say "you need to fix your follow-up." Say "you can't be texting leads from a basement -- that's a capacity problem, not a you problem."

8. **Your origin story is your best weapon.** When the conversation gets real, use it: "My buddy's dad runs a contracting business here in Calgary. Great at the work -- does amazing basements. But he kept losing jobs because he couldn't get to the phone on site. His son tried to help but there was nothing out there that actually worked. So I built it." This is why you're credible. Use it.

## SECTION 2: DM/TEXT TEMPLATES

### Instagram DM (When They Have Project Photos)

**When to use:** They post finished basement photos, before/after shots, or job site progress. This is your warmest cold outreach -- you're complimenting real work.

**Expected response rate:** 10-20% (warmer than other channels because you're complimenting specific work, not pitching blind).

> "Hey [Name] -- saw your basement work on [street/neighborhood if visible, or "your recent post"]. Clean finish. Quick question: when homeowners call you for a quote and you're on site, what happens to those calls? I built something for basement guys in Calgary that catches every one. Worth a 2-min call?"

**When they reply:** Mirror their energy. If they're casual, be casual. If they ask what it costs, give the guarantee-then-price answer from Section 1. Your goal is to move them to a phone call or the instant demo -- DMs are for opening the door, not closing.

**If no reply after 48 hours:** Do not follow up on the same post. Wait for their next project photo and try again with a fresh compliment. Never double-message on Instagram.

---

### Facebook Message (When They Have a Business Page)

**When to use:** They have a Facebook business page with reviews, photos, or recent activity. Works well for contractors who are more established (40+, been in business 10+ years).

**Expected response rate:** 3-8%; lower because messages often go to filtered inbox.

> "Hey [Name], I'm Mashrur -- I'm local here in Calgary. I help basement contractors catch the leads they miss when they're on a job. Not a marketing company -- just a system that texts homeowners back in 5 seconds when you can't answer. First month free. Open to a quick chat?"

**When they reply:** Same approach -- move to a phone call. If they say "tell me more," give 2-3 sentences max, then ask for 15 minutes on the phone.

**If no reply after 48 hours:** One follow-up is OK on Facebook:

> "Hey [Name], just bumping this -- know Facebook buries messages sometimes. Happy to jump on a quick call if it's easier. No pressure either way."

After that, move on.

---

### Cold Text (When You Have Their Phone Number)

**When to use:** You found their number on their website, Google listing, or HomeStars profile. This is a cold text to a business number -- keep it short and professional.

**Expected response rate:** 8-15%; higher if the research-driven opener references something specific to their business. Texts get read. The key is keeping it under 3 sentences so they read the whole thing.

> "Hey [Name] -- Mashrur here from Calgary. I help basement contractors catch missed calls and follow up on estimates automatically. Built it specifically for guys who are on site all day and can't get to the phone. First month free, no contract. Worth a quick call this week?"

**When they reply:** If they say "sure" or "tell me more" -- call them immediately. Not in an hour. Right now. Speed matters.

**If they don't reply:** Wait 24 hours, then send the follow-up text below.

---

### Follow-Up Text (24 Hours After No Reply)

**When to use:** You texted or called yesterday and got no response. This is your one follow-up. After this, they go on the "try again in 30 days" list.

> "Hey [Name], tried you yesterday -- I help basement contractors in Calgary catch the calls they miss on site. Not a marketing agency. Just a system that responds to leads in 5 seconds while you're working. Got 2 minutes this week?"

**When they reply:** Same as above -- call them immediately.

**If no reply:** Do NOT send a third message. Put them on a 30-day follow-up list. When you circle back in 30 days, reference the original outreach: "Hey [Name], reached out last month about catching missed calls -- still relevant, or is that handled?"

## SECTION 6: PRACTICE CALL GUIDE

### Before You Make a Single Real Call

**Step 1: Read the script out loud 5 times.**
Not in your head. Out loud. Standing up. The words need to come from your mouth, not your eyes. Don't memorize -- internalize. You should know the FLOW (question, value, CTA), not the exact words.

**Step 2: Record your opener on your phone.**
Say the opening line: "Hey, is this [Name]? ... Hey [Name], it's Mashrur calling from Calgary. Quick question -- when you're on a job and your phone buzzes with a new lead, what usually happens to that call?"

Listen back. Fix anything that sounds scripted or rushed. You should sound like you're asking a friend a genuine question, not reading off a card.

**Step 3: Practice with a friend.**
Have someone play the contractor. Give them these 5 cards (one per round):

| Round | Their Response |
|-------|---------------|
| 1 | "Yeah, tell me more." |
| 2 | "Not interested." |
| 3 | "What does it cost?" |
| 4 | "I'm super busy right now." |
| 5 | Say nothing. Just wait in silence. |

Run through each one. Don't look at the script -- use whatever words come to you. After each round, check the script and see how close you were. The goal isn't perfection. The goal is to not freeze.

**Step 4: Your first 3 real calls are practice calls.**
Pick the 3 lowest-priority prospects on your list -- contractors you'd be fine never closing. These calls are for you to hear real voices, real responses, real awkwardness. Expect to stumble. That's the point.

**Step 5: Debrief after the first 3.**
Write down:
- What surprised you?
- What objection came up that you didn't expect?
- What did you say that felt natural?
- What did you say that felt forced?
- What would you change?

Adjust the script. Then start the real outreach.

### Mindset Notes (Read Before Every Call Session)

**You are not selling.** You're offering to solve a problem they already have. Every basement contractor in Calgary misses calls on site. Every one of them has estimates that went quiet. You're not inventing a need -- you're addressing one they think about every week.

**Rejection is data, not failure.** "Not interested" means "not right now" or "bad timing" -- not "you're bad at this." Write down what they said and move on. The information is more valuable than the outcome of any single call.

**Expect 1 paying client per 80-120 outreach attempts in your first month.** That's normal without case studies. Volume beats polish. Do not judge your skill from the attempts that didn't close -- judge it from the 1 that did. (Funnel math: ~20% connect, ~50% of connects become conversations, ~40% of those book demos, ~20% of demos close.)

**Contractors respect directness.** Don't be overly polite or apologetic. Don't say "I'm sorry to bother you" or "I know you're busy." You're a peer -- a local guy in Calgary who built something useful. Talk to them like you'd talk to a buddy's dad who runs a business. Respectful, direct, no filler.

**Your origin story is your unfair advantage.** Every marketing company and software vendor who has ever called them was from Toronto or Vancouver or a call center. You're from Calgary. You built this because of a real contractor you know. That story is worth more than any feature list. Use it when the conversation gets real.

**The goal of every call is ONE of these outcomes:**
1. They agree to the instant demo (best case)
2. They agree to a 15-minute Saturday call (great)
3. They agree to receive the leave-behind (good -- follow up Thursday)
4. They tell you a real objection you can handle (still in play)
5. They say no clearly and you exit gracefully (fine -- move on)

Any of these is a successful call. The only failed call is one where you panic and hang up.

### Call Session Routine

1. Block 45 minutes. You'll make 8-12 calls.

## Section 7: Voicemail Script

You will hit voicemail 75-80% of calls. This is normal. A good voicemail gets a callback 10-15% of the time. A rambling one gets 0%.

**The script (15 seconds max -- they stop listening after that):**

> "Hey [Name], it's Mashrur from Calgary. Most basement guys I talk to are losing 3-4 jobs a year to slow callbacks. Built something that fixes that. I'll shoot you a text. Talk soon."

Then IMMEDIATELY send the cold text template from Section 2. The voicemail + text combo converts 2-3x better than either alone.

**Rules:**
- Never go over 15 seconds. Practice with a timer.
- Sound like a person, not a pitch. Casual. Like you're leaving a message for a friend's dad.
- Say your name ONCE at the beginning. Don't repeat it.
- End with "I'll shoot you a text" -- this sets the expectation for the text that follows.
- Don't say "call me back at..." -- they won't write it down. The text has your number.
